Discrimination

Good day, everyone. I am Mark Christian S. Rivera, and today I want to speak about the issue of discrimination. Discrimination is a force that prevents progress and creates division within society. It happens when people are treated unfairly because of things like their race, gender, religion, or disability, things that should never define a person’s worth or limit their opportunities. A good example of standing up against this injustice is Pedro Peláez. With his deep knowledge of law, he boldly spoke out against unfair treatment and didn’t hesitate to criticize both Spanish and Filipino priests when necessary. Peláez argued that these actions went against the Christian belief that all people are equal before God. In 1850, he published *El Clero Filipino*, a protest against these unjust practices. His actions remind us that discrimination should always be challenged. No one should be treated differently because of their background or beliefs. We must continue to fight for equality and fairness for all.
